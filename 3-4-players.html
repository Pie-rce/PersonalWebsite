3-4 Players

You are entering the realm of intermediate pianists.

alt="Intermediate Piano Level Player Playing Piano">

If you are a "Number 3" player, you should be able to play everything from the previous levels and the upcoming material with no mistakes. A "Number 3" player should be able to play the 12 minor scales one finger at a time, perform two-finger chord progressions for the 12 major scales, and play the following songs:

  • Fur Elise
  • Clair de Lune
  • Prelude and Fugue in C Major, BWV 846

Mastering these skills shows that you're entering the realm of an intermediate piano player.

When you become a "Number 4" player, you should be able to perform the techniques described for "Number 3" but with triad chords going up and down each scale. This means being able to do it in both upward and downward single-note patterns. Additionally, you should be able to play the following pieces:

  • Fantaisie-Impromptu
  • The Nutcracker Suite (Tchaikovsky)
  • Songs Without Words (Mendelssohn)

At this level, you should be able to complete these techniques and play the pieces without mistakes. Mastering the techniques for a "Number 4" player demonstrates a good understanding of basic song structure and music theory. This foundation will help you become a more advanced player in the future and set the stage for reaching "Number 5" status.
